I often struggle to define what I do. A more manageable answer often becomes what I’m working on at any given time. If you haven’t checked out my .plan file yet, that’s probably a good place to start. I update it daily, so check the commit history for that file to see changes in what I’ve been working on over time. As a matter of principle, I try to do both some hard science/engineering work and more reflective/creative work daily. Much of this doesn't make it into the .plan file, but I try to chronicle all that is measurable.

Titles are strange, but I work to earn that of “artist”. And as we know, every artist needs a studio. Mine is called A-TEMP®. It's a name that stands for many things, “After Temporary” being its primary expansion. Others possible expansions include after temps (time, [FR]), artificial temporary, artificial time, and so on... it may even be a crude attempt to spell, well, attempt. Like everything I do, the studio's name is an extension of others’ work. If you don't know the reference, hit my line and I'll tell you.

Like any good corporation, A-TEMP® has its subsidiaries. What would Coke be without Diet Coke? RROSE ENTERPRISES is my art practice. Something's Missing, LLC handles my design work and all collaborations. We have an in-house publisher in A-TEMP® Publishing and a technology and innovation wing called Turing Time Technologies (t3 for short). Underneath it all, there exists an aspirationally great management operation called A-TEMP® Logistics. Oh, and we have an art store called “Curated Vacancy”.

Make no mistake, this is a studio in spirit, not in size. As of right now, I'm the only full-time member of A-TEMP®. While you’re reading this you’re a part of the team as well. Art is an exercise in communication. Without both a sender and receiver, we are left in stasis.

Some of the things I work on don’t fall under the A-TEMP® umbrella. The prime example of this is CWDT, a research project run jointly by Charles Crabtree and myself.
